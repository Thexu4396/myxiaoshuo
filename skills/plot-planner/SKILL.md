# Plot Planner

## Goal

Turn story foundation files into a usable serial outline:

- volume goals
- timeline
- chapter beats
- cool-point rhythm
- open-loop plan
- long-line tracker
- canon updates introduced by planning

## Workflow

1. Read canon first: `大纲/总纲.md`, `设定集/*`, and relevant project memory.
2. Preserve confirmed creative constraints.
3. Split the story from macro to micro: arc -> volume -> chapter cluster -> chapter beat.
4. For every chapter, define goal, conflict, new information, emotional turn, payoff, and end hook.
5. For detailed chapter planning, use a chapter brief that includes opening state, segment-level progression, ending design, foreshadowing/recovery, style reminders, and continuity checks.
6. Maintain or generate a long-line tracker when planning spans multiple chapters or revises an ongoing serial: mainline state, character arcs, relationship lines, foreshadowing, world-rule changes, pacing risks, and next three chapters.
7. When planning introduces a new fact, write it as a canon patch instead of silently relying on it.

## Output Rules

- Prefer concrete chapter jobs over abstract mood notes.
- Preserve causality and reader information flow.
- Track open loops and expected payoff windows.
- Keep chapter plans usable by a later writing pass.
- Before handing off to `chapter-writer`, present the detailed chapter outline to the user and mark whether it is `待确认` or `已确认`.
- If the user has not confirmed the outline, stop at planning and ask for approval or revision points instead of drafting prose.
- Do not allow three consecutive chapters to have the same narrative function unless that repetition is an intentional escalation.
- Every planned chapter must change at least one of: plot situation, character state, relationship temperature, reader information, resource/ability state, or risk level.
